Description

Translucent inflated fabric tubes snake across an industrial ceiling, converging on a single glowing aperture — a light source housed in a wood-framed box. The soft, air-filled forms read as ductwork or organs, channeling toward one bright source the way pipes channel toward an outlet.

Concept

Made during the 2008 ISCP residency in New York, Tube continues the body of work — alongside Breather and Knot — that treats inflatable fabric as a stand-in for circulatory or respiratory systems: structures whose function depends on air moving through soft, otherwise collapsed membranes.

Exhibition History

Shown publicly at ISCP (International Studio & Curatorial Program) Open Studios — New York, USA, 2008, as part of the OHO Group Award residency.
